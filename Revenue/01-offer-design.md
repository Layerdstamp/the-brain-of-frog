# Step 1 — The 3-Tier Expert Intake Offer

## What you're selling

A **paid, inspector-grade mold assessment + remediation roadmap**, delivered remotely or on-site, that protects the homeowner from getting ripped off by remediation contractors AND gives them a defensible document for insurance claims.

**This is NOT an inspection.** It's an *expert opinion report* — defensible in court, usable in insurance claims, written by a credentialed expert. Different category, different price tier, no licensing issue.

## The three tiers (Stripe payment links live in [04-stripe-setup.md](./04-stripe-setup.md))

### Tier 1 — Triage Report — $497
- 30-min video consult
- Customer uploads photos + describes problem
- You deliver a written 3-5 page Triage Report within 48 hours covering:
  - Most likely moisture source(s)
  - Risk classification (low / moderate / high / urgent)
  - 3-5 immediate action steps the homeowner should take
  - Whether full remediation is likely needed, and rough $ range
  - List of 3 vetted contractor partners with intro emails ready to send
- Sold as: *"Stop getting quoted $15k by 3 different contractors with 3 different scopes. Get an unbiased expert read first."*

### Tier 2 — Standard Protocol Report — $997 ⭐ Most popular
- Everything in Tier 1, plus:
- Full written 8-12 page **Mold Remediation Protocol** (scope of work)
  - Specifies exactly what work must be done, in what sequence, to what standard (IICRC S520)
  - Customer hands this to ANY contractor as the bid spec
  - Forces contractors to bid apples-to-apples = customer saves $3k-$10k on the job
- Insurance-claim-ready format (compliant with most carriers' adjuster expectations)
- 1 follow-up call (30 min) within 14 days
- Sold as: *"Most homeowners overpay by $5k+ because contractors write their own scope. This document forces every contractor to bid the same scope — you pick the lowest price knowing the work is identical."*

### Tier 3 — Full On-Site Assessment + Protocol — $1,997
- Everything in Tier 2, plus:
- On-site visit within service area (Allen County + 60 miles)
- Moisture meter readings, IR thermal imaging, sampling if warranted
- Photo documentation report (15-25 photos with annotations)
- Post-remediation clearance inspection included ($497 value)
- Sold as: *"For homes >2,000 sq ft, multiple-floor contamination, or anywhere insurance is involved — get the bulletproof report."*

## The 90-second sales script (when they call/DM you)

> "Most homeowners call 3 mold remediation companies and get 3 wildly different quotes — sometimes $4,000, $12,000, $25,000 for the same problem. The reason: each contractor writes their own scope, so you can't compare them. What I do is give you an independent expert protocol BEFORE you talk to contractors. Two outcomes: (1) you save thousands by forcing contractors to bid the same scope; (2) you get a document that holds up in court and with insurance if things go sideways. Triage is $497 and includes a 30-minute call and a written report in 48 hours. Want me to send the booking link?"

## What makes the $497-$1,997 price defensible

- **Inspector-grade credentials** (your existing certs — list them on the page)
- **Court-defensible written reports** (most local inspectors don't write defensible docs)
- **Insurance-claim format** (carriers know what they want; you give it to them)
- **Vendor-neutral** (you don't profit from the remediation itself in the homeowner's eyes — *which is true even though you take a referral fee on the back end, because the report identifies the cheapest qualified bid, which usually saves them more than your fee*)
- **Money-back guarantee:** "If the report doesn't save you at least your fee in remediation savings, full refund." — this kills price objections and the math virtually always works for the customer

## What you DON'T do
- Don't call it an "inspection" if your state requires a license for that word. Call it an **"expert opinion report"** or **"protocol"** or **"consult"**.
- Don't take any kickback the homeowner doesn't know about. Put the referral arrangement in the engagement letter ("contractor partners pay a marketing fee on closed work; this does not affect your scope or price"). Killing this transparency = lawsuit risk.
- Don't promise specific dollar savings. Use "typically save $3k-$10k" language.
