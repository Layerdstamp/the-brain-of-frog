# Step 2 — Land 3 Contractor Partners This Week

## Why contractors will pay you 10%

Marketing-qualified mold leads cost remediation contractors **$200-$800 each via Google Ads, with a 5-15% close rate**. That means each *closed* job from paid ads costs them $2k-$5k in marketing.

You're handing them a lead that:
- Has already paid YOU $497+ (proves they have money)
- Has a written protocol (zero scoping cost for the contractor)
- Has already accepted that remediation is needed
- Trusts an expert (you) who recommended them

**Close rate on your leads: 60-80%, not 5-15%.** That makes your lead worth **$5k-$15k** to them. A 10% referral fee on the remediation job is a steal for the contractor — and a no-brainer to agree to.

## The 4-minute pitch call (script)

When the contractor owner answers:

> "Hey [name] — Alex with MonitorMyAir, building science expert here in Fort Wayne. Quick reason for the call: I'm running an expert-intake service where homeowners pay me $500-$2,000 for a remediation protocol before they hire anyone. I refer them to 2-3 vetted contractors with a complete scope of work already written. My close rate on those referrals is 60-80%. Two questions: would you be open to being one of my 2-3 partner contractors in this market, and is a 10% referral fee on closed remediation work something you can structure? Most of the contractors I'm talking to are running their own Google Ads at $400+ per lead with sub-10% close rates, so this works out a lot better for them."

If they say yes (most will), follow with:

> "Perfect. I'll send a one-page referral agreement to your email — standard stuff, kill it any time, exclusive to closed business I send you. What's the best email?"

If they push back on 10%:

> "I'm open to discussing. The math I'm using: your blended cost-per-closed-job from paid ads is in the $2-5k range. On a $15k job, my 10% is $1,500 — and I'm delivering a scoped, motivated buyer at higher close rates. That said, if 8% works better and we still go, I'll do 8% on the first 5 closes and we revisit. Fair?"

## The referral agreement (1-page template)

```
REFERRAL & PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

Between:
  [Your LLC name], "Referrer"
  [Contractor LLC name], "Partner"

Effective: [date]

1. Referrer operates MonitorMyAir, an expert mold consultation service. Referrer 
   delivers qualified mold-remediation leads with completed remediation protocols
   to Partner from time to time.

2. Partner agrees to pay Referrer a referral commission equal to TEN PERCENT (10%) 
   of the gross contract value of any remediation, removal, restoration, drying, 
   reconstruction, or related work performed by Partner for a customer originally 
   introduced by Referrer.

3. Commission is due within 15 days of Partner receiving final payment from the 
   customer. Partner will provide Referrer with a copy of the executed customer 
   contract and final invoice for verification.

4. Commission obligation applies to:
   (a) The original referred job, AND
   (b) Any additional work performed by Partner for the same customer or at the 
       same property within 24 months of the original referral.

5. This agreement is non-exclusive on Partner's side (Partner may receive leads 
   from other sources) but Referrer agrees not to refer the same customer to a 
   competing remediation provider once Partner has been engaged.

6. Either party may terminate this agreement with 30 days written notice. 
   Termination does not affect commissions owed on jobs in progress.

7. Disputes resolved in Allen County, Indiana, governed by Indiana law.

Signed:
  Referrer: _______________________  Date: ______
  Partner:  _______________________  Date: ______
```

Send this as a PDF via DocuSign/PandaDoc free tier or just email-signed.

## Quality gate — how to pick which contractors to actually partner with

Don't sign 10 contractors. Sign **3**. Quality over quantity, because every protocol you write should be deliverable to a contractor who'll do the job right.

Pick partners with:
- **4.5+ stars** on Google, 20+ reviews
- **In business 3+ years** (won't disappear)
- **Pulls permits properly** (ask: "do you pull demo and electrical permits when scope requires it?")
- **Carries proper insurance** (ask for COI)
- **Returns your call within 4 hours** during the trial period

**Drop any contractor who:**
- Argues with your scope (they want to upsell the customer themselves)
- Pays slow (>30 days from invoice)
- Has any BBB complaints in the last 12 months
